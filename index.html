    About Me

    I'm currently a PhD student in Statistics at the University of Chicago.  I've recently also become quite involved with the Chicago Data Science community through the Data Science for Social Good fellowship. I was also drawn to the fellowship because I've always wanted a platform for using quantitative processes in the betterment of humanity.

    For more press coverage, see the New York Times article on the summer program. If you're interested in joining the Chicago Data Science and Hacking Communities, take a look at the links provided in the Websites section.

    I graduated from the University of Chicago with a BS with Honors in Mathematics, BS Economics, and BA Statistics (2009).  I'm from the great state of New Jersey.
